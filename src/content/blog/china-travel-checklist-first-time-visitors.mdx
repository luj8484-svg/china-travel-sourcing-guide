Use this checklist before your first China trip. It focuses on the things that most often create friction for foreign visitors: payment, internet access, transport, addresses, and daily routing.

## 1. Prepare mobile payments

China is highly mobile-payment focused. Before you fly, install Alipay and WeChat, connect an international card if supported, and test whether your identity verification is complete.

Keep at least one backup payment method. Some hotels and larger stores accept international cards, but smaller restaurants, taxis, and local shops may expect mobile payment.

## 2. Set up internet access

Decide whether you will use an eSIM, international roaming, or a local SIM card. Short-term tourists often prefer eSIMs because they can be activated before arrival. Longer trips may benefit from a local SIM, especially if you need a Chinese phone number for deliveries or bookings.

Download offline copies of booking confirmations, hotel addresses, and passport scans before departure.

## 3. Save every address in Chinese

Save your hotel name, address, and nearby landmark in Chinese. This helps when using ride-hailing apps, asking hotel staff for help, or showing a destination to a driver.

For each day, save the Chinese names of your main stops. This small step prevents many avoidable delays.

## 4. Choose hotels near transport

For a first trip, a slightly more expensive hotel near a metro line is often worth it. In cities like Shanghai, Guangzhou, Beijing, Shenzhen, and Hangzhou, metro access can save time and reduce stress.

If you are attending a trade fair or wholesale market, choose location by route convenience, not only by distance on a map.

## 5. Keep your daily plan simple

China's cities are large. A good first-day plan has fewer stops, more buffer time, and one clear backup option. Group nearby attractions or business meetings together instead of crossing the city multiple times.

## Quick checklist

- Passport valid for your trip
- Visa or visa-free transit eligibility checked
- Alipay or WeChat Pay installed and tested
- eSIM, roaming, or SIM plan ready
- Hotel address saved in Chinese
- Transport apps installed
- Translation app installed
- Copies of bookings saved offline
- Emergency contact and embassy details saved
