The Canton Fair can be extremely productive, but only if you arrive with a focused plan. Walking the halls without a product list, target price, and supplier comparison system often leads to a stack of brochures and very little clarity.

## 1. Confirm your phase before booking

The Canton Fair is organized by product categories across different phases. Your first task is to match your target products to the correct phase. Do this before booking flights and hotels.

If you source across multiple product categories, decide whether the extra travel time is worth attending more than one phase.

## 2. Register and prepare documents

Prepare your buyer registration materials early. Keep digital and printed copies of your passport, business card, invitation or registration documents, hotel booking, and product list.

Bring more business cards than you think you need. Many supplier conversations still start with a card exchange.

## 3. Pick the right hotel area

During the fair, hotel prices rise and traffic becomes slower. A good hotel is not just "near Guangzhou" but convenient for reaching the Pazhou complex and your evening plans.

Many buyers prefer areas with metro access, restaurants, and easy routes to the fair venue.

## 4. Ask better supplier questions

Before visiting booths, prepare a standard question list:

- What is the MOQ for this product?
- Can you customize material, color, logo, or packaging?
- What is the sample cost and lead time?
- What are your payment terms?
- Do you have export experience in my market?
- Can you provide certificates or test reports if needed?
- What is the estimated production lead time after deposit?

## 5. Follow up within 48 hours

After each fair day, sort supplier notes while the conversation is still fresh. Rank suppliers by product fit, communication quality, price logic, sample feasibility, and export readiness.

The real value of the Canton Fair often appears after the fair, when you compare quotes, request samples, and filter out weak suppliers.
