Sourcing from China is not just finding a supplier. It is a process of defining requirements, comparing offers, testing samples, checking risk, and controlling quality before shipment.

## 1. Define the product before contacting suppliers

Write down your requirements before asking for quotes. Include material, dimensions, color, function, packaging, certifications, target market, order quantity, and target price range.

The clearer your request, the easier it is to compare suppliers fairly.

## 2. Compare suppliers with the same questions

Use the same question set for every supplier. This prevents you from comparing one supplier's FOB price with another supplier's EXW price, or one supplier's sample-ready product with another supplier's custom production quote.

Track these fields:

- Unit price
- MOQ
- Sample cost
- Sample lead time
- Production lead time
- Payment terms
- Trade terms
- Packaging details
- Certificate or testing availability
- Communication quality

## 3. Order samples before placing a bulk order

Samples are not only about product quality. They also test how the supplier communicates, packs products, handles small issues, and follows instructions.

Take photos, record measurements, and compare samples against your written requirements.

## 4. Understand payment and shipping terms

Common trade terms such as EXW and FOB can change your real cost. A cheap quote may become expensive after inland transport, export handling, packaging, testing, or freight is added.

Ask suppliers to clarify what is included in the quote and what is excluded.

## 5. Plan quality control before production ends

Quality control should not be an afterthought. Confirm product specifications, approved sample details, packaging requirements, labeling, and inspection timing before paying the balance.

For new suppliers or custom products, a pre-shipment inspection can prevent costly surprises.
