Getting around in China is usually manageable when the basics are ready. The hard part for first-time visitors is not that every city is difficult. It is that transport depends on several other pieces working at the same time: payment, internet, Chinese addresses, maps, and a backup route.

If one piece fails after arrival, the whole first day can feel harder. You may know where you want to go, but not have the address in Chinese. You may have a map route, but no mobile data. You may find a taxi, but not have the hotel phone number or payment backup ready.

Use this guide with the <a href="/china-travel/">China Travel Setup Guide</a> and the <a href="/tools/china-trip-checklist/">China Trip Checklist</a> before you travel.

## Who this guide is for

This guide is for visitors who need a practical city transport plan before arriving in China.

It is especially useful if you are:

- Visiting China for the first time
- Attending the Canton Fair
- Traveling for business meetings
- A small importer visiting suppliers or markets
- An overseas buyer preparing a sourcing trip
- A tourist planning your own city routes
- Visiting hotels, markets, restaurants, trade fairs, or supplier locations

## Why getting around requires preparation

Transport touches the whole trip. It affects more than the ride itself.

It can affect:

- Airport arrival
- Hotel check-in
- Metro routes
- Taxi communication
- Payment setup
- Chinese address use
- Supplier meetings
- Canton Fair commute
- Evening meals
- Emergency route changes

Transport apps, payment support, map features, and city rules can change. Before relying on one app, one payment method, or one live route, test your setup and save key details offline.

## Main ways to get around in China

Most first-time visitors use a mix of transport methods.

| Option | Useful for | What to prepare |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Metro | Predictable city movement and avoiding traffic | Station names, transfer route, payment or ticket option, and walking distance |
| Taxi | Luggage, late arrival, direct hotel trips, business meetings | Chinese address, hotel phone number, payment backup, and map screenshot |
| Ride-hailing | Point-to-point routes when app access and payment work | Mobile internet, payment setup, map pin, Chinese address, and backup route |
| Walking near hotel areas | Meals, shops, convenience stores, short errands | Practical hotel location, map screenshots, and safe route awareness |
| Airport or railway transfer | First and last trip legs | Hotel address, arrival plan, payment setup, and backup route |
| Backup transport plan | When the first option fails | Another route, another payment method, hotel contact, and power bank |

Do not plan only around one perfect route. Plan around what you will do if the first route is not available.

## Metro basics for first-time visitors

Metro systems can be useful because they are usually more predictable than road traffic. A hotel near a useful metro line can make daily movement easier, especially in large cities.

Before relying on the metro:

- Check routes before arrival.
- Save station names and transfer notes.
- Consider luggage, stairs, walking distance, and transfers.
- Allow extra time during busy hours.
- Check payment or ticket options locally.
- Save the hotel address in Chinese in case you need a taxi backup.

Transport rules, payment options, and station procedures can vary by city and can change. Verify current details locally before depending on one method.

If you are still choosing accommodation, read the <a href="/blog/china-hotel-location-guide-first-time-visitors/">China Hotel Location Guide</a> before booking a hotel far from daily transport.

## Taxi basics

Taxis can be useful when you have luggage, arrive late, travel with colleagues, or need to reach a business meeting directly.

Before taking a taxi, prepare:

- Destination name in Chinese
- Full Chinese address
- Hotel or destination phone number
- Nearby landmark if available
- Map screenshot
- Payment method and backup payment
- Your hotel address for the return trip

Do not rely only on an English hotel name. A Chinese address and phone number are much more useful if communication is difficult.

For address setup, use the <a href="/blog/how-to-save-use-chinese-addresses-china/">Chinese address preparation guide</a>.

## Ride-hailing basics

Ride-hailing can be useful, but it depends on your phone, internet, payment setup, location accuracy, and app access. Features and foreign visitor access can change, so test your setup before relying on it.

Prepare:

- Mobile internet
- Payment setup
- Chinese hotel address
- Destination map pin or screenshot
- Hotel phone number
- Backup taxi or metro route
- Power bank

Do not plan around one ride-hailing app as your only option. If it does not work at the airport, hotel, venue, or market, you still need another route.

## Maps and route planning

Maps are only useful if the saved location is correct and the app works when you need it.

Before travel:

- Save key locations before departure.
- Save hotel address in Chinese.
- Screenshot important routes.
- Confirm map pins with your hotel, supplier, or event organizer.
- Keep important addresses outside the map app too.
- Do not rely on one map app only.
- Save backup routes for airport, hotel, and main daily destinations.

Map app features and app access can change. Test your setup before relying on it, and keep important details offline.

## Airport arrival transport plan

Arrival day deserves its own transport plan.

Before landing:

- Hotel address saved in Chinese
- Hotel phone number saved
- Payment app tested
- Mobile internet ready
- Backup card or cash prepared
- Map screenshot saved
- Late arrival plan prepared
- Power bank charged

If your flight arrives late, keep the plan simple. A slightly more convenient hotel location can matter more than a small room-rate difference on the first night.

## Supplier visit and sourcing trip transport notes

Supplier visits need more address detail than ordinary sightseeing. A factory, office, warehouse, showroom, or market booth may not be easy to identify from a short English name.

Before leaving:

- Save supplier address in Chinese.
- Confirm gate, building, tower, floor, or room.
- Save contact person and phone number.
- Save map screenshot and nearby landmark.
- Plan enough travel time.
- Avoid scheduling too many distant meetings in one day.
- Keep notes for the return route.

## Transport backup plan

Use this simple backup framework:

### Primary route

Your expected route, such as metro, taxi, ride-hailing, or a hotel-arranged transfer.

### Backup route

Another way to reach the same place if the first option fails.

### Chinese address

The destination name and address in Chinese, saved offline.

### Hotel phone number

A number that a driver, station staff member, or local helper can call if needed.

### Map screenshot

A saved screenshot of the location and nearby roads or landmarks.

### Payment backup

A second payment method in case one app or card does not work.

### Internet backup

A backup data option or offline plan if your live route does not load.

### Power bank

Transport, payment, maps, translation, and contacts all depend on your phone.

### Emergency contact

A hotel, colleague, supplier, travel companion, or local contact who can help if the route fails.

## Common transport mistakes first-time visitors make

### Saving only English hotel names

English names are not always enough for taxis, maps, or local help.

### Not preparing mobile internet

Maps, ride-hailing, translation, and payment often depend on mobile data.

### Not testing payment apps

Payment support can change. Test your setup before arrival and carry a backup.

### Relying only on one map app

Save addresses, screenshots, and route notes outside the app too.

### Not checking hotel distance from daily plans

A hotel can be in the same city but still far from your real route.

### Underestimating transfer time

Large stations, long walks, luggage, and busy hours can add time.

### Not saving hotel phone number

The phone number can help when a driver or local staff member needs to confirm the destination.

### Not preparing a backup route after arrival

Arrival is the worst time to solve every transport detail from scratch.

### Scheduling meetings too far apart

For business or sourcing trips, too many distant meetings can make the day inefficient.
