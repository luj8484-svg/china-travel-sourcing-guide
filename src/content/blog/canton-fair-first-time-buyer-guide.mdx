The Canton Fair is one of the main trade fairs in China for overseas buyers. It is held in Guangzhou and organized by product categories across different phases. For a first-time buyer, the fair can be very useful, but only if you arrive with a clear product list and a simple way to compare suppliers.

Do not treat the fair like a casual walk through an exhibition. Treat it like several days of supplier interviews.

## 1. Understand the three Canton Fair phases

The fair is split into phases. The exact category list should always be checked before booking, but the broad pattern is:

- Phase 1: electronics, machinery, vehicles, hardware, building materials, and industrial products.
- Phase 2: consumer goods, gifts, home decorations, household items, and similar categories.
- Phase 3: textiles, garments, shoes, office supplies, bags, food, medical, and health-related products.

If your product category is in the wrong phase, you may miss most relevant suppliers. Confirm the phase first, then book flights and hotels.

## 2. Prepare registration and buyer badge details

The buyer badge is your entry pass. Prepare registration details early so you do not lose time on arrival.

Before travel, prepare:

- Passport information
- Business card details
- Company name and contact information
- Product categories you plan to source
- Hotel booking and travel dates
- Digital and printed copies of key documents

Bring more business cards than you expect to use. Suppliers often ask for one at the start of a conversation.

## 3. Choose a practical hotel location

During the fair, traffic and hotel prices can both increase. The best hotel is not always the closest on the map. The best hotel is one with a reliable route to the Pazhou exhibition area and enough food or transport options nearby.

When comparing hotels, check:

- Metro access to the fair area
- Morning travel time
- Evening food options nearby
- Distance to railway stations or the airport if you have a tight schedule
- Whether the hotel can provide receipts or business traveler support if needed

## 4. Plan booth visits before entering the halls

The fair is large. Without a plan, you can spend hours walking and still miss useful suppliers.

Before each fair day:

- List your target product categories.
- Mark must-visit halls or supplier booths if you have them.
- Prepare a booth note template.
- Decide what counts as a good supplier for your order size.
- Leave time for unexpected booths that look relevant.

At each booth, record the supplier name, booth number, contact person, product details, MOQ, sample terms, certificates, and follow-up action.

## 5. Ask the same supplier questions every time

Consistent questions make comparison easier after the fair.

Ask:

- What is the MOQ for this product?
- What is the sample cost and sample lead time?
- What is the production lead time after deposit?
- What trade terms do you quote, such as EXW or FOB?
- What packaging is included?
- Can the product be customized?
- Do you have export experience in my market?
- Can you provide certificates or test reports if needed?
- What payment terms do you usually accept?

If the answer is vague, write that down too. Communication quality matters.

## 6. Follow up within 48 hours

The real work begins after the fair day ends. Sort notes before you forget the details.

After the fair:

- Group suppliers by product category.
- Remove suppliers that do not fit your MOQ, price range, or quality needs.
- Request formal quotes from shortlisted suppliers.
- Ask for sample invoices and sample timelines.
- Confirm packaging, certificates, trade terms, and payment terms in writing.
- Decide which suppliers need factory visits or video calls.

Fast follow-up helps suppliers remember you and keeps the conversation specific.

## Quick Canton Fair checklist

- Correct phase confirmed
- Buyer badge and registration details prepared
- Hotel route to Pazhou checked
- Product list and target price range ready
- Booth note template prepared
- Supplier questions written down
- Follow-up system ready for quotes and samples
