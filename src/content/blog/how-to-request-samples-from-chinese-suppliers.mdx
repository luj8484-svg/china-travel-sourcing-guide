Requesting a sample is not the same as casually asking for one product. A sample is a formal test before a possible bulk order.

The sample stage should help you check product fit, materials, size, color, packaging, communication, sample cost, lead time, and what the supplier can confirm in writing. It should also help you prepare better questions before paying a deposit for a larger order.

If you are still deciding which supplier deserves a sample request, first use the <a href="/blog/how-to-compare-chinese-suppliers-before-sample-order/">Chinese supplier comparison guide</a> and the <a href="/tools/supplier-comparison-sheet/">Supplier Comparison Sheet</a>.

## What to confirm before requesting samples

Before requesting samples from a Chinese supplier, confirm:

- Exact product specification
- Sample type
- Sample cost
- Sample lead time
- Shipping cost and shipping method
- Payment method
- Packaging
- Logo, color, size, or other customization details
- Whether the sample represents mass production quality
- Bulk order MOQ and unit price range

Get written confirmation before payment. Terms can vary by supplier and product, so do not rely only on a quick chat message when cost, timing, or customization matters.

## Why samples matter before a bulk order

Samples help buyers check more than product appearance.

A sample can help you review:

- Product fit
- Material
- Size
- Color
- Function
- Packaging
- Communication quality
- Sample lead time
- Quality expectation
- Supplier follow-up reliability

But sample approval does not remove the need for bulk order quality control. A good sample is useful evidence, not a guarantee that every production unit will match it.

For new suppliers, custom products, or higher-risk orders, consider professional inspection, testing, or compliance support when needed.

After sample review, use the <a href="/blog/chinese-supplier-quality-control-checklist-before-bulk-order/">Chinese supplier quality control checklist</a> before confirming a bulk order.

## Step 1: Confirm the exact product specification

Do not request a sample with only a product name. Give the supplier enough detail to know what you expect.

Confirm:

- Product model or product type
- Material
- Size
- Color
- Function
- Packaging
- Logo or customization
- Target market
- Reference photos or drawings
- Acceptable differences, if any

If your product has safety, labeling, certification, or testing requirements, verify what applies to your destination market before ordering samples or bulk goods.

## Step 2: Ask whether the sample is available now

Not all samples mean the same thing. Ask what type of sample the supplier can provide.

Common sample types include:

- Stock sample
- Customized sample
- Pre-production sample
- Sample from current production

Ask whether the sample matches mass production quality. A stock sample may show basic product direction, while a pre-production sample may be closer to your final order. The meaning can vary by product and supplier, so ask directly.

## Step 3: Confirm sample cost

Sample cost should be clear before payment.

Ask:

- What is the sample price?
- Is there a setup, mold, tooling, or customization cost?
- Is packaging included?
- Is sample shipping included or separate?
- Can the sample cost be deducted from a future bulk order?
- Are any refund or deduction terms confirmed in writing?

Do not assume every supplier refunds sample fees. Terms can vary, and the details should be confirmed before payment.

## Step 4: Confirm sample lead time

Sample timing affects your whole sourcing schedule.

Ask:

- When can the supplier prepare the sample?
- Does customization change the lead time?
- Does lead time start after payment, artwork confirmation, or material confirmation?
- Does the supplier need final packaging artwork first?
- Can busy seasons affect timing?

If timing is important, write down the start point for the lead time. "Three days" can mean different things if payment, artwork, or material confirmation has not happened yet.

## Step 5: Confirm shipping cost and shipping method

Sample shipping can be a separate cost and may affect delivery timing.

Confirm:

- Shipping cost
- Courier or shipping method
- Estimated delivery time
- Tracking availability
- Recipient name, phone number, and address
- Whether customs or import responsibility may apply

Do not rely on a general shipping estimate for final landed cost. Costs and responsibilities can vary by product, country, courier, and shipping method.

## Step 6: Confirm what the sample proves and what it does not prove

A sample can show product direction, but it does not prove everything about a future bulk order.

A sample may help you check:

- Appearance
- Material
- Size
- Basic function
- Packaging direction
- Supplier communication

A sample does not always prove:

- Full production consistency
- Certification compliance
- Packaging at scale
- Defect rate
- Delivery reliability
- Bulk order performance

Keep this in mind before treating sample approval as the end of quality control.

For the next step, prepare written quality requirements with the <a href="/blog/chinese-supplier-quality-control-checklist-before-bulk-order/">bulk order quality control checklist</a>.

## Step 7: Ask about bulk order conditions before paying for samples

Before paying for a sample, ask about the larger order conditions. Otherwise, you may receive a good sample from a supplier whose MOQ, price, lead time, or terms do not fit your business.

Confirm:

- Bulk MOQ
- Unit price and price breaks
- Production lead time
- Payment terms
- Trade terms
- Packaging
- Inspection options
- Certification or testing needs

Payment and trade terms can vary by supplier, product, and order size. Confirm details in writing before payment.

## Step 8: Compare samples from multiple suppliers

If you request samples from more than one supplier, use the same evaluation fields for every sample.

Compare:

- Product fit
- Sample quality
- Sample cost
- Sample lead time
- Packaging
- Communication quality
- Responsiveness
- Documentation
- Follow-up priority

Use the <a href="/blog/how-to-compare-chinese-suppliers-before-sample-order/">supplier comparison guide</a> before choosing which sample result should move toward a bulk order.

## Step 9: Check sample quality

When the sample arrives, inspect it carefully and record notes before making a decision.

Check:

- Appearance
- Dimensions
- Material
- Function
- Packaging
- Labeling
- Defects
- Consistency with agreed specs
- Photos and notes
- Comparison with a reference sample, if available

Take photos from multiple angles. If you find a problem, describe it clearly instead of sending only "quality is not good."

## Step 10: Follow up after receiving the sample

The follow-up after receiving samples is where your bulk order decision becomes clearer.

Ask:

- What caused any defect or difference?
- Can the supplier revise the sample or specification?
- Do changes affect price, MOQ, lead time, or packaging?
- Which details will be used for mass production?
- Can the supplier confirm the final specification in writing?

Update your supplier comparison sheet after each sample review. Do not make the decision from memory.

## Sample request message template

Copy and adapt this message before contacting a supplier:

```text
Hello [Supplier Name],

I am interested in requesting a sample for [product].

Please confirm:

1. Exact product specification
2. Sample cost
3. Sample lead time
4. Shipping cost and shipping method
5. Whether the sample matches mass production quality
6. MOQ and unit price for a bulk order
7. Payment terms
8. Packaging options
9. Certification or testing documents if available
10. Contact person for follow-up

Please confirm these details in writing before I arrange payment.

Thank you.
```

If the sample quote leads into MOQ, trial order, or unit price discussion, use the <a href="/blog/how-to-negotiate-moq-price-chinese-suppliers/">MOQ and price negotiation guide</a> before accepting the first quote.

## Red flags before paying for samples

Be careful if a supplier:

- Avoids exact specifications
- Changes quote details repeatedly
- Gives unclear sample cost
- Gives vague lead time
- Cannot explain whether the sample matches mass production
- Pushes a bulk order before sample details are clear
- Refuses written confirmation
- Communicates inconsistently

These signs do not always mean the supplier is unusable. They mean you should slow down and confirm more details before payment.

## Common mistakes small buyers make

### Requesting samples without clear specs

If your specifications are vague, the sample may not prove much.

### Comparing different sample types

A stock sample, customized sample, and pre-production sample may answer different questions.

### Forgetting shipping cost

Sample shipping can affect the real cost and timeline.

### Not checking bulk MOQ before paying

The sample may be useful, but the later MOQ might not fit your first order.

### Not taking structured notes

Photos help, but you still need notes about defects, packaging, fit, communication, and follow-up.

### Assuming sample quality guarantees mass production

Sample quality does not always guarantee bulk production quality. Keep a written specification and plan quality control for production.

### Ordering too many samples without a comparison plan

Too many samples can become confusing if you do not track them with the same fields.
