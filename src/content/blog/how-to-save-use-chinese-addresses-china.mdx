Many first-time China travel problems are not caused by a complicated itinerary. They happen because the traveler only saved an English hotel name, a vague booking page, or a live map link that does not load when it is needed.

Chinese addresses matter for hotels, taxis, map searches, Canton Fair routes, wholesale markets, factory visits, and supplier meetings. A little address preparation before departure can save a lot of confusion after arrival.

Use this guide together with the <a href="/china-travel/">China Travel Setup Guide</a> and the <a href="/tools/china-trip-checklist/">China Trip Checklist</a> before you fly.

## Who this guide is for

This guide is for travelers who need to find places in China without depending on perfect English communication or live app access.

It is especially useful if you are:

- Visiting China for the first time
- Attending the Canton Fair
- Traveling for business meetings
- A small importer visiting suppliers or markets
- An overseas buyer preparing a sourcing trip
- A tourist booking your own hotels
- Visiting suppliers, wholesale markets, showrooms, or trade fairs

## Why Chinese addresses matter

An English hotel name is not always enough. Some hotels, office towers, markets, and venues have similar names. Some locations are easier to identify by district, nearby landmark, entrance, building, gate, tower, or floor.

You may need a Chinese address when:

- Leaving the airport for your hotel
- Showing a driver where to go
- Asking hotel staff for help
- Searching a map app
- Returning from a restaurant or market
- Visiting a trade fair venue
- Meeting a supplier or factory contact
- Arranging delivery, pickup, or courier help

Address preparation should sit next to payment setup, internet access, and hotel location. If one of those fails, a saved Chinese address can still help someone understand where you need to go.

## What address details to save

For every important place, save more than one detail.

- Place name in English
- Place name in Chinese
- Full Chinese address
- City and district
- Hotel, office, or building phone number
- Nearby landmark
- Map pin or screenshot
- Booking confirmation
- Supplier contact person if relevant
- Booth number or meeting location if relevant
- Notes about entrance, lobby, gate, tower, or floor

Address formats can vary, and app features can change. Verify important addresses with your hotel, supplier, host, or event organizer before relying on them.

## Hotel address preparation

Your hotel address is the most important address to prepare before arrival.

Before departure:

- Ask the hotel for the Chinese name and full Chinese address.
- Save the hotel phone number.
- Save the booking confirmation as a screenshot.
- Keep both English and Chinese versions.
- Check whether the hotel has similar names in the same city.
- Save a nearby landmark if the hotel provides one.
- Save a map screenshot if possible.

Do not rely only on the hotel brand name. Large cities can have many branches, and similar hotel names can create confusion.

If you have not chosen a hotel yet, start with the <a href="/blog/china-hotel-location-guide-first-time-visitors/">China Hotel Location Guide</a> so the address you save is also practical for arrival, taxis, maps, and daily routes.

## Airport arrival and taxi use

Prepare your hotel address before landing. Arrival is not the right time to search through email while tired, offline, or low on battery.

Before leaving the airport, make sure you can show:

- Hotel name in Chinese
- Full Chinese address
- Hotel phone number
- Booking confirmation screenshot
- Nearby landmark if available
- A backup screenshot in case mobile internet fails

If communication is difficult, showing the Chinese address is usually easier than trying to explain the hotel name in English. Ride-hailing and map app features can change, so test your setup before relying on one app or one live map link.

## Using addresses with map apps

Map apps and app access can vary. Test your setup before arrival and do not depend on one app only.

Before traveling:

- Save map pins before travel if possible.
- Screenshot important locations.
- Save route notes for airport to hotel and hotel to key destinations.
- Confirm the location with the hotel, event organizer, or supplier.
- Save the Chinese name and address outside the map app too.
- Keep a backup internet plan.

For internet preparation, use the <a href="/blog/china-esim-internet-access-guide/">China eSIM and Internet Access Guide</a> before departure.

## Canton Fair address notes

Canton Fair visitors should prepare addresses before fair days, not while standing in a hotel lobby with a tight schedule.

Save:

- Hotel address in Chinese
- Canton Fair venue address from official sources
- Fair phase and visit dates
- Meeting points
- Booth notes
- Supplier contact details
- Return route to hotel
- Hotel phone number

## Supplier and factory visit address notes

Supplier visits can be easier to get wrong than hotel trips. Factory parks, office buildings, warehouses, markets, and showrooms may have multiple gates, towers, or entrances.

Before the visit, ask the supplier to confirm:

- Company name in Chinese
- Full factory, office, showroom, or market address
- Contact person
- Phone number
- WeChat or email if applicable
- Meeting time
- Gate, building, tower, floor, or room
- Nearest landmark
- Transport notes
- Whether the address is for the office, showroom, factory, or warehouse

For the meeting itself, prepare your questions with <a href="/blog/supplier-questions-to-ask-at-canton-fair/">Supplier Questions to Ask at the Canton Fair</a>. If you are comparing several suppliers, record addresses and contacts in the <a href="/tools/supplier-comparison-sheet/">Supplier Comparison Sheet</a>.

## Offline address backup plan

Use this simple backup plan:

### Hotel address screenshot

Save the Chinese hotel name, full address, and phone number as a screenshot.

### Chinese address text copied into notes

Keep copyable Chinese text outside your map app so you can paste or show it quickly.

### Phone number saved

Save the hotel, supplier, or event contact number in your phone and in offline notes.

### Map screenshot

Take a screenshot of the map pin and nearby roads or landmarks.

### Booking confirmation

Keep your hotel booking, meeting confirmation, or event details available offline.

### Emergency contact

Save a person or organization you can contact if directions fail.

### Power bank

Keep your phone charged. Addresses, maps, translation, payment, and messaging all depend on it.

### Backup internet option

Prepare mobile internet and a backup data option before arrival.

## Common address mistakes first-time visitors make

### Saving only the English hotel name

An English name may not identify the right branch, building, or entrance.

### Not saving the Chinese address

Drivers, hotel staff, suppliers, and local helpers may need the Chinese version.

### Not saving screenshots offline

Live maps and booking apps may not load when your data is weak or your battery is low.

### Not saving the hotel phone number

The phone number can help a driver, local staff member, or travel companion confirm the location.

### Confusing hotels with similar names

Check the district, street, and branch carefully before travel.

### Relying on one map app only

App access and features can change. Keep the address in notes and screenshots too.

### Not checking district or city

Large cities can have similar street names, hotel names, or business districts.

### Not confirming supplier visit address before departure

Ask whether the meeting is at an office, factory, showroom, market booth, or warehouse.
